Anatomy of a Colour

Colour is made of 3 major components, which are Hue, Saturation/Chroma and Brightness/Value. These make up the HSV colour system. Alternatively, you could use other systems like RGB or CMY, but HSV has always been the most intuitive to me.

Hue

Hue is what you normally think of when you hear the word colour. Red, purple, blue, green, yellow and orange are all examples of hue. It's the radial part of the colour wheel.

Value/Brightness

Value refers to how light or dark the colour is. The brighter it is, the more value.

Saturation

This has alwasy been the most confusing element in my opinion, but simply put, saturation is the intensity of colour. If you're painting traditionally, you can also think of it as the concentration of pigment in your paint.

An important point

While most people might think that hue is the most important element of colour, that is typically not true. Our eyes are adapted to prioritise differentiating values over hueshues. In fact, many artists work in greyscale beofre adding hue and saturation. Having good value structure can help give clarity to a painting.

This is the reason why even though I'm partially colourblind, I can still have decent colours.
