Colour Symbolism

It goes without saying that colours can represent a lot of different emotions or ideas. While these are never truly set in stone, it's useful to know some common symbolisms for different colours.

White represents purity and innocence. It is the brightest colour of all and therefore gives a feeling of clarity and cleanliness.

Black has many meanings depending on context, but often can represent mystery, fear and sometimes death. Think about how you were probably scared of the dark when you were little. On the other hand, as the least glaring colour, it can sometimes be comforting. Context is important.

Red is a common example of colour symbolism. It can represent intense emotions like passion and anger, and is often associated with danger because blood is red. Think about how villains often have a black and red colour scheme.

Brown is an earthy colour. It gives a sense of peace and groundedness. It is a humble and simple colour, familiar to the poor as the colour of their clothes.

Yellow is bright and happy. It represents youth, light, spring, and all things traditionally optimistic. However, like all other colours, it can also represent less appealing things. Yellow is also the colour of sickness, like jaundice.

Green is obviously representative of nature and plants. Our eyes are even hardwired, in a sense, to see more green than any other hue. Green is calming and familiar. It might also represent greed and wealth as it is also teh colour of money.

Blue is another very calming colour. We see blue all the time in the sky. It can represent safety and hope. Blue is also a traditionally very sad colour. the entire genre of blues is named after this concept. It is melancholic and slow

For historical reasons, purple is often used to represent royalty. Purple is a colour that we don't see quite as often as something like green or blue, so it can represent something foreign. In some cases, purple is also used to represent an infection or sickness
